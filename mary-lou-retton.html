    Marry Lou Retton is a retired American gymnast. She won the gold medal in the individual all-around competition at the 1984 Summer Olympics in Los Angeles. MLR also won two silver medals and two bronze medals.

    Best known as MLR is quite famous as an athlete for her outstanding performances and was the first woman in America to win a gold medal in gymnastics in 52 years. In January 2020, MLR was the first woman inducted into the Houston Sports Hall of Fame.

    As a gold medalist, she rose to fame and secured many advertisements and roles in television shows and movies, which must have accumulated a hefty amount. As of 2023, Mary’s net worth is estimated at $8 million.

    Besides that, she is quite outspoken politically and supported President Ronald Reagan.

    Lou is one of the most famous gymnasts and has received several opportunities because of it. Mary appeared in several commercials for well-known companies and industries.

    She has also made appearances in movies and television series.

    The athlete received criticism and bad press for standing against a bill protecting young children from sexual abusers. Many have called MLR out for standing against other reforms in the USA Gymnastics.

    Before learning about the American gymnast’s life and career details, here are some quick facts about her.

    Early Life, Family, And Education

    Mary Lou Retton was born in Fairmont, West Virginia, to parents Ronnie Retton and Lois Retton and is the youngest of five children.

    Her father was a former collegiate basketball player who played in a minor league. He ran a coal-industry transportation equipment business.

    Both of MLR’s parents were active and very enthusiastic about sports and physical activities.

    Hence, she took dance classes when she was only four. Then she enrolled in gymnastics classes with her sister Shari Retton.

    The former athlete was hard-working and very excited about gymnastics.

    She used to sleep in her gym attire the night before her classes at West Virginia University. Similarly, she was inspired by legendary gymnasts such as Olga Korbut and Nadia Comăneci.

    When Mary saw them perform on television, she knew she wanted to be a gymnast and win gold medals.

    As a result, Retton committed to gymnastics and trained under famous gymnastics coach Béla Károlyi.

    He has trained many Olympic champions and was inducted into the International Gymnastics Hall of Fame in 1997.

    Although he is known for his tough-love coaching style, he got gentle when he arrived in America. She thrived under the Romanian-American coach and continuously explored her potential.

    Retton attended Fairmont Senior High School. However, she did not graduate, as she was more focused on gymnastics.

    The gold medalist moved to Houston, Texas, to train at the Károlyi Gym. She also briefly attended the University of Texas after retiring from gymnastics.

    Age, Height, And Weight

    The former gymnast turned 55 years old as of 2023.

    She takes great care of her health and diet. Hence, despite being in her 50s, she is very fit. Mary is 1.45 meters tall and weighs 93 lb.

    Retton has brown hair and dark brown eyes when describing her physical appearance. Unfortunately, her body measurement, shoes, and tattoos (if any) are unavailable now.

    The gold medalist belongs to American nationality with Italian-American ethnicity and follows the Christian religion.

    Gymnastics Career

    Initial Career

    The former athlete started training to become a star gymnast when she was only eight and was enrolled at the University of West Virginia. Little did Lois know Mary would grow up to pursue it as a career.

    Initially, she trained under Gary Rafaloski in her hometown. However, she felt like she could train better, so she moved to Houston, Texas.

    Retton trained under Béla and his wife, Márta Károlyi; under her coaching, she improved overall as a gymnast.

    At 15, she won the American Cup and ranked second in the U.S. Nationals.

    In 1983 MLR missed the World Gymnastics Championships due to a wrist injury.

    But she also won the American Cup, Nationals, and U.S. Olympic Trials, and the gymnast won the 1983 and 1984 American Classic and Japan’s 1983 Chunichi Cup. 

    Before the 1984 Summer Olympics, she underwent knee surgery to treat her leg injury. Although she feared she might miss the Olympics, the gymnast recovered quickly.

    In the first Summer Olympics held in America after 52 years, MLR won her first gold medal. Furthermore, she became the first American woman to win an all-around gold medal.

    She performed an ankle vault, and for the final vault, she beautifully performed the very difficult Tsukahara vault.

    America’s Sweetheart also won two silver and bronze medals in the Summer Olympics. Popular sports magazine, Sports Illustrated, named her “Sportswoman of the Year.”

    Post Gymnastic Career

    After winning her third and final American Cup all-around competition in 1985, Mary retired in 1986. After that, she had little experience in movies and television.

    Retton appeared in the comedy movie Scrooged, and popular TV series like Glee and Baywatch and was a contestant in 27th season of Dancing With The Stars in 2018.

    MLR also had a children’s series called Mary Lou’s Flip Flop. Besides that, she utilized her time to support President Ronald Reagan’s re-election. She was also a part of the 2004 Republican National Convention.

    USAG Abuse Scandal 

    The former athlete’s name was juggled in 2017 when the USGA’s sex abuse scandal was all over the news. Female athletes accused gym owners, coaches, and staff of sexually abusing them.

    It is one of the largest sex abuse scandals in sports history. The biggest face of the scandal was former osteopathic physician Larry Nassar. Furthermore, he is accused of abusing over 250 women.

    Most of the women were minors at the time of the abuse. As a result of his actions, the USAG received many lawsuits for ignoring complaints, cover-up, and enabling Nassar.

    Many people called for reforms and proper laws in the gymnastics governing body of the United States.

    Hence, Senators Dianne Feinstein and John Thune sponsored a bill. It was called the Protecting Young Victims from Sexual Abuse and Safe Sport Authorization of 2017 bill.

    The bill protects young athletes from sexual abuse. Moreover, it also promotes a safe environment for their growth.

    Along with the USAG, Mary met with Senator Feinstein to convince her not to pass the bill. Subsequently, she was heavily criticized for supporting the organization instead of the abuse survivors.

    The former pair have four daughters: Shayla, McKenna, Skyla, and Emma Kelley.
