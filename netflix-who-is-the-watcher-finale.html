    Netflix's The Watcher: Ending explained

    A STALKER wreaked havoc on a neighborhood as one couple scrambled to find out the creeper's identity. As Netflix viewers reached the conclusion of The Watcher, they ended up with even more questions than answers.

    25 Mar 2024, 00:00

    A STALKER wreaked havoc on a neighborhood as one couple scrambled to find out the creeper's identity.

    As Netflix viewers reached the conclusion of The Watcher, they ended up with even more questions than answers.

    What happened in The Watcher's finale?

    During the season finale of The Watcher, the Brannock family move out of their New Jersey home at 657 Boulevard, Westfield.

    Nora and Dean, played by Oscar nominee Naomi Watts and Blonde actor Bobby Cannavale, sell their home to Jennifer Coolidge's character Karen.

    As the Brannocks attempt to move on, Nora sees success again in her pottery career.

    However, Dean can't let go of the mystery as he continues to wonder which one of his family's neighbors is the unidentified individual that called themselves The Watcher.

    Content with how the family's new life away from the harassment is going, Nora gives Dean an ultimatum: Either give up his obsession with solving The Watcher's identity or give up the life they rebuilt together.

    Dean seemingly decides to forego any chances of finding out who The Watcher is and the Brannock family's private detective Theodora Birch dies from cancer.

    Meanwhile, Karen moves out of 657 Boulevard and another family moves in.

    Next door, Pearl Winslow, Big Mo, John Graff, Roger Kaplan, and some other neighbors continue to reconvene for The Preservation Society's meetings.

    When the new family moves in, it is implied to the audience that the neighborhood's frightening behavior of stalking will endure.

    Who is The Watcher?

    Although the Brannock family moved out of their former home at 657 Boulevard and escaped The Watcher's harassment, the tangled web still resulted in confusion as the stalker's supposed identity implicated multiple characters.

    In the penultimate episode, Nora and Dean thought they unmasked The Watcher when they concluded that Karen's duplicity in real estate could only mean one thing.

    They assumed Karen masterminded the Brannocks' harassment with Detective Rourke Chamberlain.

    The husband and wife stand are even more convinced when Karen contacts her friends at Page Six in an effort to foil the family's plans to sell the home.

    After The Watcher stalks Karen and forces her to sell the home, the suspicion turns away from her and Detective Chamberlain.

    Dean seemingly gets all of his questions answered when his former private detective Theodora confesses to being The Watcher.

    Before she succumbs to cancer, Theodora admits to stalking Dean and Nora and that working with them provided the perfect cover-up.

    When Theodora's daughter discloses to Dean and Nora that her mother only confessed to being The Watcher in order to give the Brannocks some peace from the mayhem, the late investigator's revelation is debunked.

    As the season came to an end, it is implied that Dean was the one to run Karen out of 657 Boulevard.

    Thus, leading fans to believe Dean was The Watcher all along.

    After all, he did send one of the earlier letters and feigned innocence until his wife caught him.

    All of the theories then point to Nora when she is seen observing Dean from a distance, implying she is The Watcher.

    With audiences left questioning whether Nora, Dean, Karen, or Theodora were The Watcher, it's hard to remember that the other suspects also include:

    • Pearl Winslow and her brother Jasper
    • Big Mo and her husband Mitch
    • Dakota, the alarm system technician
    • John Graff
    • Roger Kaplan

    Regarding the cliffhanger ending, Naomi Watts explained the possibilities surrounding the theories of Nora and Dean as The Watcher to EW.

    The actress mentioned to the outlet: "They feel like the house is going to solve their problems and it ends up being the catalyst that causes a whole lot of new problems that they didn't anticipate.
