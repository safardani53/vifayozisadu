    Lonzo Ball’s Professional Career | Stats

    Highlighting the professional journey of Lonzo into basketball is quite impressive and surreal.

    After playing for his college and school, he tried for National Basketball Association in the year 2017.

    And the basketballer didn’t let his people down as he got drafted by Los Angeles Lakers. The Los Angeles Lakers selected Lonzo with the second overall pick.

    Although he was a rookie for the season, he did not lose his magic in paying games.

    Lonzo showed his active participation in the 2017 NBA Summer League by averaging 16.3 points, 9.3 assists, 7.7 rebounds, 2.5 steals, and 1.0 blocks per game.

    Plus, the game he played on 20th October 2017 helped him score a career-high 29 points, going along with 11 rebounds and nine assists.

    2018- 2019 Season

    In the beginning, Lonzo planned to play all 82 games as a sophomore but, his left knee didn’t support him as he went through arthroscopic surgery.

    This uneven incident didn’t let Lonzo participate in full-contact practices. And sadly, his injury history does not stop just there!

    After playing almost 47 games, Lonzo again got injured in his ankle. As a result, he was informed to be sidelined for about four-six weeks.

    Also, he underwent surgery that the Lakers did not authorize. The Lakers informed him that his contract could be voided if he proceeded and was later taken out. As a result, he became entirely out in March 2019.
