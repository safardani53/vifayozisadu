    Body Measurements

    Max Scherzer has an athletic body – he stands tall at 6 feet 3 inches (1.9 meters) while weighing 200 lb (100 kg). 

    Likewise, he depicts a biceps measurement of 15 inches, a chest size of 44 inches, and a waist size of 35 inches. Moving on, he wears of shoe size of 12 (US). 

    Workout 

    Most of Max’s typical workout season usually includes lifting four times a week, separating his upper and lower body. Well, he works out on his upper and lower part on alternate days of the week.

    To elaborate, Mondays and Thursdays are kept for his heavy lower body workout, while Tuesdays and Fridays go for heavy lower body exercise. Then, for the rest of the days, he indulges in a light-body workout. 

    In addition, Max’s exercises include lunges, squats, power lifts, abdominal and core circuits, step-ups, and many more. 

    “The favorite workout exercise for me is the squats. They’re tough, but they’re so worth it. If I can squat heavy and not get sore the next day, I know my legs are strong.” – Max Scherzer

    According to the sources, Max Scherzer does not have a fixed diet routine and eateries. Hence, he intakes most of the things that give him nutritional value.

    Physical Appearance

    Moving on, Mac then joked and asked them also to inspect his former teammate Bryce Harper’s hair. 

    Not to mention, Max portrays an uncommon eye condition named Heterochromia Iridis. It is a rare condition due to which he has two different eye colors: brown on his left eye and blue on the right eye. 

    Max Scherzer | Early Life 

    Scherzer (fully named Maxwell Martin Scherzer) was born on July 27, 1984, under the star sign Leo, to his parents, Jan Scherzer and Brad Scherzer. Well, he was born and raised in the St. Louis suburb of Chesterfield, Missouri. 

    Max grew up with a love for sports alongside his sibling brother, Alex Scherzer. However, his one and only brother died by suicide early in the summer of 2012. According to the sources, Alex took his life after struggling with clinical depression. 

    During his life, Alex was immensely interested in analyzing advanced baseball statistics (sabermetrics). Hence, Max also ended up learning the skill and tools. Likewise, since Alex’s death, Scherzer dedicates every start to his late brother.

    Education 

    Talking about his educational background, Max Scherzer attended Parkway Central High School in his hometown. Ever since his early days, Max was invested in sports and grew up as a St. Louis Cardinals fan.

    Moving on, he then played baseball, football, and basketball with the school team. Following it, Max enrolled at the University of Missouri in Columbia.

    Max Scherzer | Career 

    Scherzer Showed early interest in the athletic side, and hence, many had eyes on him. Before the start of his collegiate days, the Cardinals’ scouting director had already selected Max for the 2003 Major League Baseball (MLB) draft. 

    During Max’s early career days, he trained with multiple renowned faces of the baseball industry, including Tony Vitello, Tim Jamieson, and many more. During his collegiate years, Max had bagged the 2005 Big 12 Pitcher of the Year Award. 

    Right from the beginning of his collegiate years, Max had played with multiple professionals. He initially made his debut with the La Crosse Loggers of the Northwoods League (NWL). 

    Arizona Diamondbacks

    Max made his first career debut with the Arizona Diamondbacks during the 2006 MLB draft as the eleventh overall pick. Before the Diamondbacks, he had played with the Fort Worth Cats of the independent American Association. 

    Two years later, in 2008, Max made his professional debut as a reliever. Indeed, he had an impressive debut as he was just one short of the MLB record for most in a debut relief appearance. Not long after, Max recorded his first career loss as a starter which was closely followed by his foremost career win. 

    Detroit Tigers 

    In 2009, Max Scherzer joined the Detroit Tigers as part of a three-team agreement. His throwing skill also improved quite a lot. By next year in 2010, Max played for the Triple-A Toledo Mud Hens. 

    Right with them, he pitched into the ninth inning for the first time in his career. Max had a smooth career line with small injuries at the side. However, by 2013, he had bagged his first Cy Young Award. 

    The same year in September, Max also became the first major league pitcher to reach 20 wins. Then, he bagged the Players Choice Award for AL Outstanding Pitcher and the Baseball Digest Pitcher of the Year. Altogether, Max stayed with the Tigers till 2014. 

    Washington Nationals 

    At the start of the year 2015, Max Scherzer signed a seven years contract with the Washington Nationals. He debuted as the starting pitcher with the team and earned his Career’s 100th win. 

    Back then in October, Max also gave non-continuous title wins and awards. The next year in 2016, he had his second CY Youth Award and yet another title in 2017. Likewise, Max stood as the first pitcher to strike out 20 batters in a regulation game. 

    Not long after, Scherzer became the third-fastest pitcher to reach 2,000 strikeouts. By 2018, he was the top pitcher with over 300 strikeouts under his name.

    After several notable achievements, Max Scherzer played in the World Series and grabbed his foremost championship. Similarly, he stayed with the Nationals till 2021. 

    Los Angeles Dodgers 

    The Nationals traded Max Scherzer to the Los Angeles Dodgers, with whom he reached 3,000 strikeouts. By his end time with the Dodgers, he was titled the Sporting News NL Starting Pitcher of the Year and Players Choice Award winner for NL Outstanding Pitcher. 

    New York Mets  

    In December 2021, Max Scherzer signed a contract with the Mets; however, he only made his game debut in April 2022. 

    Injuries

    Throughout his professional career, Max Scherzer has faced multiple injuries on the field. Among them, Max’s first injury came in 2019, which was a broken nose after a ball struck him in the face while bunting in batting practice.

    Before this, in August 2017, Max struggled with neck spasms and hence, stayed out of the games for several days. 

    The same year, Max then spent his time on the injured list after his back injury. Back then, he had suffered from bursitis in his right scapula and a strain on the right rhomboid muscle in his upper back.

    Max Scherzer | Awards And Achievements 

    From the starting days of his professional career to his current self, Max Scherzer has improved tons on his pitching skill. Today, he throws five pitches which are a four-seam fastball (92-96 mph), a slider (85–86 mph), a changeup (84–85 mph), a cutter (88–89 mph), and a curveball (78–79 mph). 

    Among these, Max mostly uses a fastball-slider combination against right-handed hitters and a fastball-changeup-cutter combination against left-handed hitters. Not to mention, he also gained his nickname “Mad Max” due to his intense pitching style. 

    Some of Max Scherzer’s achievements and awards to date are listed as below. 

    • 8× All-Star (2013–2019 and 2021)
    • Baseball Digest Pitcher of the Year (2013 and 2021)
    • World Series champion (2019)
    • 2× All-MLB First Team (2019 and 2021)
    • 3× Cy Young Award (2013, 2016, and 2017)
    • 4× wins leader (2013, 2014, 2016, and 2018)
    • 3× NL strikeout leader (2016–2018)

    • The Sporting News Starting Pitcher of the Year (2013, 2016, 2017, and 2021)

    Apart from being a baseball pitcher, Max Scherzer is a married man with a lovely family. He is currently married to his longtime girlfriend, Erica May-Scherzer. The duo first met while attending the University of Missouri, where Erica was also a pitcher on the softball team. 

    After a good friendship, the couple started dating in 2005 and took vows in 2013. The duo tied the knot in November of that year in Scottsdale, Ariz.

    After their wedding, they visited New Zealand and Fiji and had fun with bungee jumping, mountain biking, and helicopter touring.
