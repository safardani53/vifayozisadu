    Carly Rae Jepsen is a Canadian singer, songwriter and actor. Born and raised in Mission, British Columbia, Jepsen played several leading roles in her high school musical productions and studied musical theater at the Canadian College of Performing Arts in Victoria, British Columbia. . After completing her studies, she moved to Vancouver and then competed in the Canadian Idol's fifth season in 2007, finishing third. In 2008, Jepsen released his folk-sounding debut studio album, Tug of War, in Canada. Jepsen's breakthrough came in 2012, when his single "Call Me Maybe" gained widespread popularity; The song was the best-selling single of that year, reaching number one in 18 countries. As a result, she signed a worldwide recording contract with School Boy Records and Interscope Records. Jepsen's second studio album, Kiss, was released later that year.
