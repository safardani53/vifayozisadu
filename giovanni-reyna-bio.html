    Giovanni Reyna | Early Life & Career

    Giovanni Alejandro Reyna was born on November 13, 2002, in Sunderland, England. However, young Reyna had to move away from England at a very young age because his father, Claudio, decided to sign for the New York Red Bulls.

    Subsequently, following his father’s footsteps, young Gio enrolled in the New York City FC youth academy to provide better facilities.

    Thereafter, he spent his whole teenage years with the academy making himself one of the top prospects in the entire nation.

    In fact, Giovanni even helped his youth side win the 2017 Generation Adidas Cup and be awarded the best player in the tournament.

    Added to that, the 17-year-old also helped the U.S U15’s to the prestigious Torneo Delle Nazioni Cup win.

    Giovanni Reyna | Professional Career

    A host of top European teams wanted to sign the young prodigy not only because of his huge potential but also because he was a free agent.

    But in the end, it was German giants Borussia Dortmund who managed to secure the service of the talented 17-year-old in January 2020.

    Since then, the attacking midfielder has gone on to make 27 appearances for Dortmund’s U-19 team and the senior team. Likewise, in those 27 appearances, Reyna has managed an impressive nine goals and nine assists.

    Furthermore, some memorable moments of the young American’s time in Germany include him breaking the youngest ever American record to play in the Bundesliga at 17 years and 66 days.

    Interestingly, the record was previously held by Chelsea’s tricky winger, Christian Pulisic.

    And, if Gio is to follow in the footsteps of Pulisic, then we are in for a treat. However, that is not the only record that the young attacking midfielder has broken.

    In fact, Reyna became the youngest goalscorer in German Cup history when he scored against Werder Bremen in a 2-3 defeat on February 4, 2020.

    Furthermore, the 17-year-old also holds the record for being the youngest American to play and record an assist in a Champions League fixture.

    To illustrate, Giovanni came on in the 57th minute against Paris Saint Germain in a Champions League round of 16 ties.

    After nine minutes, Reyna set up another wunderkind, Erling Haaland, for the match-winning goal, which put them 2-1 up going into the second leg.

    But unfortunately, Dortmund wound up losing the second leg as they could not contain Kylian Mbappe and Neymar‘s likes.
