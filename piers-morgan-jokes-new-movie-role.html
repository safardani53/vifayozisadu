    19 Sep 2024, 00:00

    PIERS Morgan has "revealed himself" as Daniel Craig's replacement as James Bond ahead of the star's final 007 appearance.

    The TV host joked he had landed the movie role as he posed in a suave suit on a night out with his wife Celia - channelling the reputable film hero.

    The new picture comes hot on the heels of the TV star revealing he was joining The Sun and News Corp in a huge global deal.

    Piers, 55, and his other half were out on a date night at the launch of the Burlington Arcade 007 installation launch.

    The former GMB host couldn't help poking fun by suggesting he could be the next James Bond - as he posed in front of the sign, suited and booted.

    He wrote alongside the comical snap: "Daniel Craig’s replacement as 007 finally revealed…"

    Fans loved this cheeky caption, with one writing: "Move over Idris!" while another wrote: "You could pull it off, as well, Piers! Good luck to you!"

    Meanwhile, last week we revealed how Piers is returning to his roots to present a new global TV show and be a columnist for The Sun.

    He will bring his vast experience and bold opinions to The Sun to tackle the big issues in his new regular column for our website and newspaper.

    He will host a brand spanking new TV show to air weeknights in the UK, US and Australia.  He will also present a series of True Crime documentaries. 

    more on piers

    Piers, who previously co-presented Good Morning Britain, said that his new show will be a "fearless forum for lively debate" which will "celebrate the right of everyone to have an opinion".

    Announcing the news, Piers rejoiced that he’s “coming home” – while The Sun's Editor-in-Chief Victoria Newton hailed it a "bigger comeback than Ronaldo".

    Speaking about his career move, Piers: “I’m thrilled to be returning to News Corp, which is where I began my media career more than 30 years ago.

    "Rupert Murdoch has been a constant and fearless champion of free speech and we are going to be building something new and very exciting together.

    “I want my global show to be a fearless forum for lively debate and agenda-setting interviews, and a place that celebrates the right of everyone to have an opinion, and for those opinions to be vigorously examined and challenged.

    “I’m also delighted to become a columnist for The Sun and the New York Post, two brilliantly successful and popular newspaper brands.

    “I’m going home and we’re going to have some fun."

    Piers Morgan’s new show will air on talkTV in the UK, FOX Nation for US audiences, and on Sky News Australia. 

    The show will be produced and filmed at News UK’s studios in London Bridge.
