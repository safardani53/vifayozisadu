    18 Apr 2024, 00:00

    - The fans in the utility room and bathroom were inoperable and had to be replaced.

    - The boiler kept "tripping off", with wires cut. The thermostats upstairs and downstairs were broken, with no hot water and no heating on either floor.

    - The radiators were not installed level, there was a leaking towel rail in the main bathroom, and cement poured down the cloakroom toilet.

    - The master bathroom is always cold, Lucy says.

    - The knob fell off in the master shower, and the shower head is not installed correctly, with water spraying all over the ceiling.

    - The master bathroom door does not close, and the guest bathroom door does not open.

    - The master wet room extractor fan leaks, leaving stains on the ceiling - and similarly, the family bathroom extractor vent leaks, also leaving stains on the ceiling.

    - The master shower head was blocked with pieces of wood, and the master shower drain tap had cement in it, meaning it could not be cleaned and the bathroom flooded.

    -The drains were blocked in the master bathroom, and the master shower filled with toilet waste, Lucy claims.

    - In the guest bathroom, the hot and cold taps are installed backwards.

    - The guest en-suite shower drain was not attached, with water pouring through the kitchen ceiling.

    - There was a leak in the entrance hall ceiling, with the drywall needing to be replaced.

    - Outside, the tap at the front of the house was leaking, and the tap at the rear of the house was not caulked.

    - The front porch is still not finished, Lucy says - the post on the right is loose and not attached properly, due to the wrong bricks used.

    - In the garage, water leaks from above the tank on the rear wall, with water seeping around tank and up through the foundation.

    - Lucy says there is no power in the garage, with the power tripping off if the outlet is used.

    - The outside drains were not sealed.

    - Finally, the front downstairs bay window was cracked, and not correctly sealed, leaving a gap between the window and the brickwork on the outside.
