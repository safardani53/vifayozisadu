    Cheeky mum blasted by other parents after sharing huge stash of Ellas Kitchen pouches she swiped

    A MUM has been blasted by other parents after sharing the massive stash of Ella's Kitchen pouches she nabbed from the Butlin's buffet for her child. She took to the Extreme Couponing and Bargains UK group on Facebook to share a picture of the 23 packets she'd picked up from her holiday.

    02 Feb 2024, 00:00

    A MUM has been blasted by other parents after sharing the massive stash of Ella's Kitchen pouches she nabbed from the Butlin's buffet for her child.

    She took to the Extreme Couponing and Bargains UK group on Facebook to share a picture of the 23 packets she'd picked up from her holiday.

    "If you go on holiday to Butlin's and have their dining plan they provide you with Ella's Kitchen pouches for free," she wrote.

    "We had the premium package and you also the staff and they provide them for you.

    "Not bad considering how much you can pay in the shop for them!

    "Perfect to start weaning and on the go when you are with them

    Read more Parenting stories

    "We collected a few for our little ones."

    She concluded: "Just a little to people to get the most out of it."

    The bigger pouches retail for around £1.30 each, while the smaller ones are £1 each.

    The post quickly sparked fury from other parents, with one commenting: "So you just stole a load then, not sure that's a 'bargain'."

    Most read in Fabulous

    "She hasn’t stolen them, they hand them to you over the dinner/breakfast counter if they see you have a small child," someone else hit back.

    "Butlin's food courts are cheap enough and it's all you can eat!" a third wrote.

    "This is so cheeky to also take loads of pouches home!

    "They should defo put them behind the counter and only allow a certain amount to people."

    "They were behind a counter when we were there last week, I wondered why but this post has enlightened me," someone else commented.

    As another wrote: "Butlins Minehead, they are behind the counters now and you have to ask for them.

    "You get given two at breakfast time and two in the evening tea time. I think they've done it this way because people were just grabbing far too many and not leaving enough for others."
