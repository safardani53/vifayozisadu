    Takeoff, also known as Kirshnik Khari Ball, was assassinated on November 1, 2022, at 810 Billiards & Bowling in Houston, Texas. According to TMZ, Ball was playing dice with Quavo when a dispute erupted, murdering the rapper. According to the same reports, officers arrived at the crime site at 2:30 a.m. Ball was declared dead at the scene, and two other persons were wounded and hospitalized, according to Houston Police. According to the most current event reports, a video of Quavo on the phone with a 911 operator asking, “What does she need to do?” has gone viral. The caller presents herself as a nurse and offers to assist Ball. TMZ has obtained an audio tape in which dispatchers discuss the event and state that five bullets were fired.

    Takeoff’s personal life and dating history

    Throughout his great career, Ball was never spotted with a lady, hinting that he was not seeing anybody before to his death. However, he catapulted to popularity when it was revealed in 2017 that he was seeing Katy Perry. Ball initially appeared with the artist in 2017 at a celebration. The accusations grew when Migos and Perry worked together on Perry’s tune Bon Appetit and performed on Saturday Night Live. However, neither Ball nor Perry acknowledged the report, with Ball claiming on his Instagram page at the time that he was officially single. He later tweeted a photo with the caption “Loner.” He subsequently erased the message, though.

    In 2018, it was reported that Ball was dating rapper Dreamdoll. The story, however, went away as the people concerned kept mute. Rubi Rose, Heather Rose, and Lakeya Darshay have all been associated to Ball. However, no proof has been shown to support these allegations, and they are all presently simply hearsay.

    All About Takeoff

    Takeoff grew up in Lawrenceville, Georgia, and joined Migos with Quavo and Offset. The duo started rapping in 2008, and their debut production was a 2011 mixtape named Juug Season. In June 2012, Migos released their second mixtape, No Label. The trio rose to prominence with their song Versace and released their first album, Yung Rich Nation, in July 2015. They rose to prominence with another single, Bad and Boujee, starring Lil Uzi Vert.

    Migos subsequently followed up with two additional albums, Culture and Culture II, both of which debuted at the top of the Billboard 200. The Last Rocket was released in November 2018, while Culture III will be released in June 2021. Meanwhile, it was reported last month that Takeoff had broken up after Quavo’s ex-girlfriend Saweetie reportedly slept with Offset. The following month, Quavo and Takeoff released their joint album, Only Built for Infinity Links, putting the allegations to rest.
