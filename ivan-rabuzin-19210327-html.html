    Ivan Rabuzin is a naive Croatian artist. French art critic Anatole Jakovsky described him in 1972 as "one of the greatest naive painters of all time and of every nation". Rabuzin's father was a minor, and Ivan was the sixth of his 11 children. Ivan worked as a carpenter for many years and only started painting in 1956, when he was thirty-five years old. He was not formally trained as an artist, but his first solo exhibition in 1960 was a success and he changed careers, becoming a professional painter in 1962. His 1963 at the Mona Lisa Gallery in Paris was the beginning of its growth in international prestige. Rabuzin's art is characterized by dense geometrical patterns of vegetation and clouds forming rich structures in the form of arabesques painted with gentle pastel colors. Its motive has been described as an "ideal reconstruction of the world".
