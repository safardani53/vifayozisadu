    A SLAVE gang led by a dad nicknamed The Tank Commander have been jailed for a savage 24-year campaign of kidnappings, violence and intimidation against terrified labourers.

    One terrified victim was tortured naked in a pigsty and made to eat dog food while another was told he would be skinned alive with a razor.

    The McPhee family preyed on vulnerable men who came from broken or troubled backgrounds and promised them work such as monoblocking driveways and cutting trees.

    A total of eight victims had to do mammoth shifts for little or no pay and were brutally punished if they complained or attempted to escape the gang's clutches.

    One slave told a jury he was kept naked in the shed, made to eat dog food and told to drink his own urine if he was thirsty.

    Yesterday Bobby McPhee, 65, his sons James, 45, and Steven, 37, and son-in-law John Miller, 38, were jailed for a total of 29 years.

    Passing sentence at the High Court in Glasgow, judge Lady Stacey said the slave gang preyed on men with a "variety of problems".

    She said: "There was violence over a long period.

    "You forced people away from their families to work for you.

    "Evidence showed an attitude of thinking you were able to tell people what to do, when to do it, where to live and backing up instructions with violence."

    Bobby McPhee, known as The Tank Commander, was jailed for ten years, son James got nine years and Miller got seven years after they were convicted of charges including assault, abduction and holding men in "servitude" from 1992 to 2016.

    Steven McPhee admitted one assault and was jailed for three years.

    The gang shouted inaudible comments as they were led down from the dock.

    During the trial, one tortured victim told how he was battered and "taught a lesson" when he tried to get away.

    Another said he was "owned" by the family.

    Jurors heard further grim evidence of a petrified worker "left cowering like a dog" due to the abuse.

    One man who fled was told he had a Â£5,000 bounty on his head and would be "skinned" with a razor unless he returned.

    The trial came after a large-scale police probe that began in 2016.

    They latterly had a base at Curryside Piggery in Shotts, Lanarkshire. The crimes also took place in Glasgow, Paisley, Manchester and London.
