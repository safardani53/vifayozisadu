    Why did Southern Charm star Cameran Eubanks leave the show?

    CAMERAN Eubanks announced that she was leaving Bravo's Southern Charm ahead of its season 7 premiere in October. The reality star was in the midst of defending rumors about her husband before she let audiences know on social media that she would not be returning.

    01 Jan 0001, 00:00

    CAMERAN Eubanks announced that she was leaving Bravo's Southern Charm ahead of its season 7 premiere in October.

    The reality star was in the midst of defending rumors about her husband before she let audiences know on social media that she would not be returning.

    Who is Cameran Eubanks?

    Cameran Eubanks is a 37-year-old real estate agent from South Carolina.

    She joined Southern Charm when the show debuted back in 2014.

    She was on Real World: San Diego in 2004 and following that she competed on MTV's The Challenge.

    With a whopping 1.3million followers on Instagram, she was known on the show for her calm demeanor.

    Why did she leave Southern Charm?

    Fans have speculated Cameran announced her departure from the reality series over the impact it was having on her personal life.

    In the last season of Southern Charm Cameran, 36, accused Kathryn Dennis, 28, of spreading rumors that her husband, Dr. Jason Wimberly, had a two-year affair with a local makeup artist named Rebecca Leigh Wash.

    Cameran quit the show following the allegations, though she claimed her departure wasn’t because of the feud.

    The reality star has also been open about wanting to spend more time with her family.

    Cameran seemed excited about the upcoming season when she spoke to Us Weekly at BravoCon in November.

    She shared: “It’s nuts. It’s been really fun and interesting to watch everybody grow in different ways."

    Cameron went on: "Season seven is going to be a very high opening season in terms of, you will see that some people have made some big strides in growth and some have stayed the same. 

    After her husband's affair rumors circulated, she swiftly made the decision not to return to the drama.

    Cameran wrote on Instagram of her departure: “My decision was made and given to Bravo months ago and had absolutely nothing to do with ridiculous and fake rumors about my marriage. Please disregard any fabricated rumor. It’s a ploy for ratings and that’s it. Now I absolutely know I was smart to leave the party early. My gut has never failed me."

    “What upsets me the most is that my husband, who is the most sincere and faithful human I know is being falsely dragged into this. It disgusts me. He always supported me filming the show even though he wanted no part of the spotlight.”

    Cameron's show departure follows co-star Kathryn apologizing after she was accused of sending racially insensitive remarks to radio host Tamika Gadsden.

    What is she doing now?

    By the looks of her social media, it seems Cameran is enjoying family life and working on her own personal endeavors since she left the reality world.

    She regularly posts adorable snaps of her and her husband's three-year-old daughter Palmer.

    She announced that she would be releasing a book titled One Day You'll Thank Me in February 2021.

    Why did Thomas Ravenel say about her feud with Kathryn Dennis?

    In now-deleted tweets, former Bravo star Thomas, 57, defended ex-wife Kathryn by slamming Cameran.

    He wrote in tweets grabbed by The Ashley’s Reality Roundup that Cameran is "terrible."

    Thomas added: “Anyone who falls for Bravo editing BS has a very small brain.”
