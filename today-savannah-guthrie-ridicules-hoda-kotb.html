    TODAY star Savannah Guthrie has appeared to ridicule her co-star Hoda Kotb following an on-air slip-up.

    Kotb agreed that it had been a “long week” after making the slight gaffe on Friday.

    In an audio clip that's gone viral, Today Show radio producer Holly Palmieri wished the two hosts a “Happy Friday” before they were asked if they had any superstitions.

    Kotb asked: “Are you suspicious?” before she was corrected.

    She was meant to say the word "superstitious."

    A person in the clip can be heard saying: “It’s been a long week,” and Kotb agreed as she said: “Yes it has.”

    The audio segment was captioned: “Superstitious … and suspicious!”

    Guthrie shared the clip and posted it alongside a crying with laughter emoji.

    Kotb and Guthrie were not the only stars that were quizzed about Friday the 13th.

    In another segment, Palmieri asked fellow Today star Craig Melvin if he was superstitious.

    He replied: “I’m usually not,” before admitting that the 1970s track Superstition is his favorite Stevie Wonder tune.

    Melvin urged Palmieri to “keep her head on a swivel."

    Meanwhile, Al Roker reportedly gave the pair a “devilish look.”

    Melvin warned: “This is when the evil doers come out to play.”

    In the funny exchange, Palmieri claimed that Roker is known for apparently “terrifying” his co-star Melvin.

    Melvin said: “I think, hopefully, you know coz (sic) he has this new lease of life, I hope he left it in his previous life.”

    It came just days after Roker appeared to ridicule Melvin during a segment about money.

    Melvin revealed that he had been put on a “cash diet” so he'd become more careful with money.

    He told Today viewers: “I could only spend cash for seven days and I actually gained some cash.”

    Roker joked that Melvin’s achievements were “amazing”, adding: “Craig doesn’t normally reach in for that wallet.”

    He then shouted: “Those T-rex hands," before imitating the dinosaur.

    'T-REX HANDS'

    Guthrie was left in hysterics following the moment and said that Roker is “back.”

    Meanwhile, Kotb was seen chuckling to herself in the background.

    Roker made a morbidly funny comment on Wednesday after the show played a montage from the Golden Globes ceremony.

    He quipped: “Maybe I’m dead and this is hell” as he pointed to a whole morning show set during a live TV moment.

    Several A-listers offered their well-wishes to Roker, who was previously hospitalized with blood clots.

    Roker returned to Today on January 6 – around two months after he was first hospitalized with blood clots.
