    Who is Cory Althoff?

    Cory Althoff, from Los Angeles, is the husband of influencer and podcast host Bobbi Althoff.

    He is a senior vice president in the software development programme at CompTIA.

    After graduating with a degree in political science, Cory taught himself how to code - eventually landing a job at eBay.

    Cory has written two books - The Self-Taught Programmer and The Self-Taught Computer Scientist.

    When did Bobbi and Cory Althoff get married?

    Bobbi was born on July 31, 1997.

    Cory proposed to Bobbi on October 11, 2019, when she was 22 - and they got married sometime afterwards.

    Do they have any children?

    Bobbi has two daughters, both of whose names have been kept private.

    The social media star has joked that the girls' names are Richard and Concrete.

    Cory and Bobbi's daughters often appear on her social media feeds.

    What is Bobbi Althoff known for?

    Bobbi is a social media influencer and aspiring podcast host who first rose to fame on TikTok.

    She began posting videos on the social media site in 2020 and has since gained over 4.3million followers.

    The star has also grown a large following on other sites like Instagram, where she boasts over 861,000 followers.

    Known for her comedy clips, Althoff has used her platforms to document everything from her engagement and pregnancy to celebrity encounters and career moves.

    Since starting The Really Good Podcast, Althoff has interviewed multiple stars.

    Among those stars are YouTuber Marco Summers, who goes by Funny Marco, actor Rick Glassman, and rappers Drake and Armani White.

    Her interview with Drake went viral ahead of the episode's release after she posted a clip of their seemingly awkward conversation online.

    "I don't know any other questions," she told the Degrassi alum before asking: "Do you have any other questions?"

    Drake replied: "Not really, it's probably best you skedaddle home to be mother of the year, right?"

    Althoff responded by asking for a plane ticket home, to which Drake said: "I can do that, it's the least I could do."

    The podcast host then asked if the flight could be nonstop, and Drake thought it was a reference to his 2018 hit, Nonstop.
