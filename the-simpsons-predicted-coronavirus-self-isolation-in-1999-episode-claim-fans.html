    THE SIMPSONS fans have claimed that the animated series "predicted" coronavirus self-isolation in a 1999 episode.

    The long-running cartoon has a reputation for foreshadowing world events, including Donald Trump being elected president.

    ⚠️ Read our coronavirus live blog for the latest news & updates

    Now some believe that news anchor Kent Brockman in season 10 episode 11 episode, Wild Barts Can't Be Broken, prophesied British newsreader Krishnan Guru-Murthy's fate over 20 years ago.

    In the episode, Kent reports the news from his own living room, telling the residents of Springfield: "This is Kent Brockman reporting from my own home in accordance with the new curfew for anyone under 70."

    Earlier this week, 49-year-old Krishnan presented the news from his own living area at home as he went into self-isolation amid the growing global pandemic.

    Krishnan Channel 4 news viewers: "Good evening from my home tonight", with floral wallpaper and homely ornaments in clear view behind him.

    One shocked Simpsons fan shared grabs of both Kent and Krishnan to Twitter, writing: "I hate to say it, I really do, but The Simpsons has... I can’t actually."

    The tweet racked up hundreds of likes and retweets, with many sharing their own disbelief at the parallels.

    Prime Minister Boris Johnson has been encouraging Brits to practice social-distancing and self-isolation all week as the UK government tries to get on top of the health crisis.

    Yesterday, the PM ordered all pubs, clubs, bars, restaurants, leisure centres, cinemas, and theatres to close in a bid to prevent the killer virus from spreading further.

    As a result, Brits across the country are working from home - including Krishan and other celebrities.

    Despite the uncanny similarities between Krishnan and Kent this week, The Simpsons didn't quite predict what is going on to the same level of detail as it has done in the past.

    While both were forced to present the news from their living rooms, there is no implication of a deadly virus in the cartoon.
