    Atlanta‘s Brandi Mallory from Extreme Weight Loss has died following what fans believe to be a suspected car accident. Tributes are pouring in for the reality TV star whose loved ones and fans call her a “beautiful angel”. Clark Atlanta University, where she studied, held a candlelit vigil in her honor.

    Brandi Mallory was featured on ABC‘s Extreme Weight Loss season 4. The freelance make-up artist embarked on a significant transformation journey after reaching 329lb (149kg). Sadly, Brandi, who encouraged so many viewers to embrace their bodies, died on November 9 in Stone Mountain, Georgia.

    Brandi Mallory on Extreme Weight Loss

    Brandi starred in season 4 episode 7 of the weight loss show. As a child, Brandi fell in love with beauty pageants after watching them on television with her mother and competed in pageants for years.

    By the time she was 12 years old, Brandi weighed more than 250lb, and comment cards from pageant judges told her over and over that she was too heavy to compete, and ended up reaching 329lb.

    Brandi became trainer Chris Powell’s first client to compete in a Half Ironman, a brutal challenge that forced her to swim, bike, and run a combined 70.3 miles in under eight hours, and lose a pound a day.

    Tributes pour in for ‘beautiful angel’

    After hearing about Brandi’s passing, a multitude of posts and well-wishes were left on her social media page. Many who have recently seen her at a homecoming event are in shock.

    One fan said: “You were a light and inspiration to all. Heartbroken to hear of your passing.” Another said: “God gained a beautiful Angel! I remember meeting you, it was like we’d known each other for a lifetime.”

    A fellow fan commented on her social media page: “My gosh 💔😢 she was a light on my feed, can’t begin to imagine her effect in person. 🙏🏾🕊️ Rest in Peace, Beautiful 🩷.”

    Brandi Mallory died on November 9

    Brandi Mallory from Extreme Weight Loss was suspected to be in a car accident, but no definitive cause of death has been given. She was 40 years old when she died on November 9, 2023.

    A memorial service will be held on Sunday, November 19, 2023, from 12 pm to 6 pm at Tri-Cities Funeral Home in Georgia. Her last Instagram post was uploaded just six days before her death.
